AML Compliance Policy v3.2

Document Reference: POL-AML-2025-v3.2
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Next Review: January 1, 2026
Classification: INTERNAL — GLOBAL_AUDIT

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes the Anti-Money Laundering compliance framework for all operations. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties acting on behalf of the institution.

AML Compliance Policy v3.2 is currently in effect alongside the APAC Data Privacy Framework.

2. CROSS-BORDER TRANSACTION MONITORING

Enhanced due diligence is required for all cross-border wire transfers above USD 10,000. This includes:
- Mandatory screening against consolidated sanctions lists updated weekly
- Verification of originator and beneficiary information
- 24-hour escalation window for flagged transactions
- Quarterly reporting to the regional compliance officer

3. SCREENING REQUIREMENTS

All transactions must be screened against:
- UN Security Council Consolidated List
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals List
- EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List
- Local regulatory watch lists (HKMA, MAS, JFSA)

Screening parameters must be calibrated quarterly to reflect current FATF guidelines.

4. ESCALATION PROCEDURES

Flagged transactions must be escalated within 24 hours to the designated compliance officer. Failure to escalate within the required window constitutes a policy breach and must be reported to Internal Audit.

For transactions exceeding USD 100,000, additional approval from the Regional Head of Compliance is required before processing.

5. REPORTING CADENCE

- Monthly: Transaction screening statistics to local compliance
- Quarterly: Suspicious Activity Reports to regulators
- Quarterly: Reports to regional compliance officer on cross-border activity
- Annually: Full AML program review to Audit Committee

6. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

All staff in client-facing and operations roles must complete:
- AML refresher training: annually, mandatory completion by Q2 each year
- Enhanced due diligence workshop: upon joining and every 2 years
- Regulatory update briefings: as issued by Compliance
